Festivals and Celebrations

Experience the rich culture and vibrant traditions of Rajasthan.

Year-Round Jaipur Festivities

Jaipur Literature Festival (JLF) - (Click Here)
The Greatest Literary Show on Earth

A five-day event attracting Nobel laureates, Pulitzer prize winners, and global authors for readings, discussions, and debates.

  • When: Usually January/February
  • Speciality: Free entry, intellectual discourse.
  • Reviews: "A vibrant, global hub for book lovers."

Gangaur Festival - (Click Here)
Worship of Goddess Gauri

Celebrated with great pomp and show, dedicated to the Goddess Gauri (Parvati). Women dress up and take out processions with decorated idols.

  • When: March/April (18 days after Holi)
  • Speciality: Traditional music, dance, and colorful processions.
  • Reviews: "A spectacular display of Rajasthani culture and devotion."

Teej Festival - (Click Here)
Monsoon Celebration

A festival welcoming the monsoon, marked by women wearing green clothes, singing traditional songs, and the procession of Goddess Teej.

  • When: July/August (Monsoon season)
  • Speciality: Swings (Jhoolas), bangles, and the main Teej Procession.
  • Reviews: "Vibrant colors everywhere, a beautiful tradition."
